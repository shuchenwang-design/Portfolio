Living the fast-paced life of a NYC accountant, Yan Li treats her weekends as sacred. While she is financially comfortable and loves exploring the city's experimental art scene, she is 'time-poor.'

Yan Li doesn't schedule performances just to fill time. She demands quality. For her, a bad performance isn't just a waste of money; it is a waste of her limited recovery time. This makes her risk-averse. She feels the need to research extensively before buying a ticket to ensure the experience is worth it.

However, her busy schedule makes deep research impossible. She currently relies on scattered group chats and Discord channels for recommendations, but sifting through the noise is exhausting. She needs a way to cut through the clutter and find trusted, high-quality signals quickly.

As a busy professional with high standards, I want to view curated lists of materials (trailers, casting, reviews) in one place, so that I can validate the quality of a performance without opening ten different browser tabs.

As a discerning audience member, I want to see what my friends or tastemakers are bookmarking, so that I can discover hidden gems based on opinions I actually trust.

As someone with a tight schedule, I want to see which of my friends are interested in the same events, so that I can propose a plan without the fear of being rejected or going alone.

"The music is great, but the best part is arguing about the conductor's tempo over drinks afterwards. I miss having someone to do that with."








Andrew is a walking contradiction: a PhD candidate in Molecular Biology who is arguably more passionate about Chopin than chemistry. Having played piano since childhood, he possesses a deep, technical appreciation for music.

Recently relocated to Baltimore for his doctorate, Andrew finds himself in a 'cultural vacuum.' While his lab schedule is flexible—driven by experiments rather than strict class times—his social circle consists mostly of other science students who don't share his artistic interests. He misses his hometown routine of attending the local symphony with friends who understood the nuance of a performance.

Currently, Andrew skips many events simply because he hates going alone. For him, the joy of a performance isn't just in the watching; it's in the debrief—the spirited discussion and critique over drinks afterward. Without that social connection, the experience feels incomplete.

As a newcomer to the city, I want to find a group of people interested in the same niche performances, so that I can build a social circle outside of my lab colleagues.

As an amateur musician who loves to analyze technique, I want to join a discussion group specific to a performance I just watched, so that I can share my critique and hear other perspectives.

As someone with a flexible but unpredictable schedule, I want to see which events have 'open groups' I can join last minute, so I don't have to miss a show just because I didn't plan weeks in advance.

Users frequently cancel plans because they cannot find companions for niche performances, leading to missed cultural experiences.

Visualize friends' availability and upcoming plans to identify opportunities to join

Automatically track and notify users when friends express interest in the same events

A dedicated space to finalize plannings without switching to external messaging apps.

The pre-show research process is overwhelming, with casting info, reviews, and trailers scattered across the web.

A unified interface organizing all essential performance resources into customizable lists

A smart ranking system that highlights the most helpful resources based on user consensus.

Allow users to save, clone, and follow resource lists created by trusted tastemakers.

Users lack a dedicated space for deep, meaningful discourse after a performance, often feeling isolated in their opinions.

Private or semi-private groups tailored to specific genres or shows for focused debate.

Enable users to leave time-stamped or context-specific comments within reviews, fostering dynamic conversation threads.

Ripple is a social-driven performance app designed to bring audiences closer, therefore enriches the experience before and after a performance, creating an environment where shared experiences ripple outward acrosspersonal networks.

Performance experiences expand through interpersonal influence and flow like waves traveling outward from a single touchpoint.

The performance journey is an ongoing cycle encompassing pre-show exploration, post-show reflection, and repeated participation.

Every encounter with a performance adds a new layer to the experience, creating a network of connections, discoveries, and shared moments.
